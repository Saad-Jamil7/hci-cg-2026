What is HCI? (Page #3–4)

Human–computer interaction has roots in disciplines like ergonomics and human factors. It became a distinct field as computers became widespread.

HCI involves the design, implementation and evaluation of interactive systems in the context of the user's task and work.

Reference: Dix et al., p. 3-4

Short-Term Memory & Chunking (Page #29–30)

Short-term memory has limited capacity: about 7 ± 2 items. More accurately, we remember 7 ± 2 chunks—meaningful groups of information.

Design implication: Group related items into chunks so users don't have to memorize long lists.

Reference: Dix et al., p. 29-30

Fitts' Law (Chapter 1)

The time to hit a target depends on its distance and size. This is formalized as T = a + b log₂(D/S + 1).

Design implication: Make important controls larger and place them closer to reduce selection time.

Reference: Dix et al., Chapter 1
